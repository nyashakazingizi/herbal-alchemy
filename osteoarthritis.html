Osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative joint disease that occurs when the cartilage that cushions the ends of bones deteriorates over time. This leads to pain, stiffness, and swelling in the affected joints, making movement more difficult. OA is the most common form of arthritis, and it typically affects the knees, hips, hands, and spine.
