High cholesterol (also called hypercholesterolemia) refers to having too much cholesterol in the blood. Cholesterol is a fat-like substance that is essential for building cell membranes and producing hormones, bile acids, and vitamin D. However, when cholesterol levels are too high, it can lead to serious health problems, particularly related to the cardiovascular system.
